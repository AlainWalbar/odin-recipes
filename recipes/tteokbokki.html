Tteokbokki

Description

It is made with rice cakes (garaetteok, cylinder-shaped rice cake), fish cakes, boiled egg, and seasoned with chile paste. The rice cakes are chewy and tender. It looks super spicy and, originally, it is! But you can control the amount of chile paste. You might be able to find it easily at food vendors on the street of Korea. There are always delicious dukbokki boiling and ready to go. Garnish with sesame seeds if you like.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound Korean rice cakes (tteok), cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 pound fish cakes, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 pound boiled eggs, peeled and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1/2 cup gochujang (Korean hot pepper paste)
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup mirin
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup sesame seeds

Steps

  1. Combine gochujang, 1/2 cup water, sugar, soy sauce, mirin, and 1/2 cup water in a saucepan over medium heat. Bring to a boil, stirring constantly. Reduce heat to low and simmer until sauce thickens, about 10 minutes. Remove from heat and set aside.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add rice cakes and fish cakes and cook, stirring occasionally, until lightly browned, about 5 minutes. Add eggs and cook, stirring occasionally, until lightly browned, about 5 minutes. Add gochujang sauce and cook, stirring constantly, until sauce thickens, about 5 minutes. Remove from heat and sprinkle with sesame seeds.

Tips

It's quite spicy. So check the taste when you add the sauce so that you can control the amount of heat.

If you use frozen rice cakes, just defrost them in the microwave for 30 seconds. Don't boil the rice cakes for too long, otherwise they will be mushy.
